## 1. FIELD OF THE INVENTION
The present invention relates generally to network routing, interconnect topologies, and Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) architectures. More particularly, the invention relates to a system and method for generating Hamiltonian decompositions and logical reasoning chains in multi-dimensional toroidal graphs (e.g., $\mathbb{Z}_m^k$) using stateless algebraic logic, thereby eliminating the need for search-based pathfinding, neural weight tables, and exponential compute allocation.

---

## 2. BACKGROUND
In multi-node computing systems and cognitive architectures, efficient pathfinding and logical deduction are critical. Conventional systems rely on:
1.  **Search Algorithms / Probabilistic Inference:** Which are computationally expensive, consume massive power, and fail to scale to millions of nodes or complex logical dimensions.
2.  **Memory-Based Tables:** Such as routing tables or neural weights, which consume significant on-chip memory (SRAM/VRAM) and increase latency.

There is a significant need for a solution that is both deterministic, $O(1)$, and topologically grounded.

---

## 3. SUMMARY OF THE INVENTION
The invention, termed **Fiber-Stratified Optimization (FSO)**, utilizes the cohomological structure of symmetric graphs to reduce routing and reasoning to $O(1)$ hardware logic. By stratifying the state space into fibers and applying a symmetry-breaking "Spike Function," the system generates valid logical pathways and Hamiltonian cycles algebraically. This enables **Topological General Intelligence (TGI)**, where truth is a topological invariant and paradoxes are identified as parity obstructions.

## 4. EXPANDED CLAIMS

### Claim 1: Stateless Algebraic Routing
A method for routing data in a multi-dimensional symmetric interconnect comprising a plurality of nodes $V$, the method comprising assigning each node a coordinate in a $k$-dimensional space of modulus $m$; calculating a local fiber sum $S$; and determining a next-hop direction $D$ via a stateless permutation mapping $\sigma(S, j)$ calculated in real-time without reference to a routing table.

### Claim 2: The Universal Spike Function
The method of Claim 1, further characterized by a "Spike Transform" applied to a singular spatial column, breaking local cycle symmetry to enable global Hamiltonian closure across the entire torus.

### Claim 3: H^2 Parity-Based Paradox Detection
A method for logical truth verification, comprising:
(a) evaluating the grid size $m$ and dimensionality $k$ of a logical hypothesis;
(b) identifying a logical paradox as an $H^2$ Parity Obstruction (e.g., even $m$, odd $k$ in a 3D fiber-stratified map);
(c) refusing compute allocation to hypotheses that violate the Law of Dimensional Parity Harmony.

### Claim 4: O(1) Cognitive Deduction via Closure Lemma
A method for logical reasoning, comprising:
(a) projecting a high-dimensional problem into a quotient fiber space via a Short Exact Sequence (SES);
(b) calculating an absolute truth density $N_b(m) = m^{m-1} \cdot \phi(m)$;
(c) executing logical jumps via a Stateless Spike Synapse in $O(1)$ gate delays without neural weights.

### Claim 5: Dimensional Escapism for Logic Resolution
A system for resolving logical obstructions by escalating a hypothesis from an obstructed dimension $k$ to a harmonious higher dimension $k'$, wherein the parity alignment (e.g., even $m$, even $k'$) restores topological feasibility.

### Claim 6: Stateless Cognitive Hardware (The TGI Kernel)
A hardware architecture comprising a plurality of logic gates configured as a toroidal manifold, a parity checking circuit for instant truth evaluation, and a stateless inference gate array for $O(1)$ reasoning, providing a zero-memory alternative to statistical AGI.
